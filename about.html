Background

1927-1991

Jacqueline S. Casey was born in Quincy, Massachusetts. She graduated in 1949 from Massachusetts College of art with a BFA in fashion design and illustration. After graduating Casey worked within fashion illustration, advertising and interior decorating. In 1955 fellow alumni Muriel Cooper recruited Casey to work for the MIT publications office. When Cooper left MIT in 1972 Casey took position as director of MIT office of publications

MIT

During her work as director of publications at MIT, Casey designed many of the publicity posters for MIT events. She was well known for using eye-catching imagery and bold typography to draw the audience into reading the small informational text. Her design inspiration included Karl Gestner, Armin Hoffman and Josef Muller-Brockmann

Exhibitions

* MIT

* Chelsea School of Art in London

* London College of Printing.

Legacy

Casey's work is held in the permanent collections of the library of Congress, the Museum of Modern Art in New York and the Cooper Hewitt Museum
